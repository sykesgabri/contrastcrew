Eye Protection

Sunglasses don't count.



Eye protection is incredibly important and should absolutely not be glossed over. The consequences of not using proper eye protection can be your eyesight, and that is something you absolutely must keep in mind, you should always wear proper eye protection at every nerf event you attend, and if you host any nerf events you should make proper eye protection a mandatory rule. Sunglasses do not count as proper eye protection. Pictured to the left of this text is an example of regular non-eyepro sunglasses that were hit by an FVJ out of a modified blaster. (Thank you to kerb23 or u/caschrock for providing permission to use this image.) As you can see, the sunglasses did not do anything to stop the dart, and as a result, if anyone was wearing said sunglasses, they very likely could've lost an eye.


So if sunglasses aren't good, what are? Well, a good choice would be something that is properly impact rated, "EN-166 B / ANSI Z87.1+" is a good choice for the majority of events, this offering from OutOfDarts meets those specifications, and is perfectly affordable.
